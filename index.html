Made for field and sampling teams

Mark points, sample polygon features, and generate field-ready outputs in QGIS

Field Guide helps you register important locations, generate marks inside polygon features, open Google Maps routes, and create mobile-ready PDF reports with route and point links. It also lets you delete selected marks and use spatial sampling methods designed for field workflows.

Less rework, clearer sampling layouts, and more confidence for the team in the field.

Why use it daily

Designed for area inspections, sampling guidance, and field routines that need clear map-based outputs.

Area inspection

Mark locations with possible issues and keep a clear record for evaluation and follow-up.

Sampling guidance

Generate better-distributed marks inside polygons to guide collection and field sampling routines.

Field operations

Share routes and PDF outputs with the team and open everything on mobile during field work.

Main features

A practical set of tools for polygon sampling, route review, and field reporting.

✓Generate centroid marks or up to 10 sample marks per polygon feature.
✓Choose spread-optimized, systematic grid, or zigzag transect sampling.
✓Delete a selected mark directly from the live session list.
✓Export and import CSV with capture order and WGS84 coordinates.
✓Open Google Maps routes with automatic chunking for longer sessions.
✓Generate mobile-friendly PDF reports with route and point links.

How it works

A direct workflow for field teams that need to move from map review to execution fast.

Mark, import, or sample

Click on the map, import a CSV, or generate marks inside polygon features with centroid, grid, spread, or zigzag methods.

Review and adjust

Review the live session list, delete a selected mark when needed, and export CSV to share the same route with your team.

Navigate and share

Open route in Google Maps and generate an organized PDF for mobile consultation.

Polygon sampling for field routines

Generate marks inside each polygon feature using methods designed for practical spatial distribution in the field.

✓Use 1 mark per feature for centroid-based workflows.
✓Use 2 to 10 marks per feature for denser field sampling.
✓Spread optimized prioritizes strong spatial coverage for irregular areas.
✓Systematic grid follows the feature shape with more even internal spacing.
✓Zigzag transect follows a field-style serpentine pattern that considers feature size and geometry.

CSV to standardize field work

Use CSV files to move points between planning, execution, and review without repeating manual entry.

✓Export with columns order, longitude, and latitude.
✓Import with WGS84 limit validation (latitude -90..90, longitude -180..180).
✓Decimal parsing with dot or comma for mixed data sources.
✓Post-import summary showing loaded points and ignored invalid rows.

PDF designed for real field usage

The final document is designed for quick reading with clean visuals and route and point links for on-the-move usage.

✓Area map for immediate visual context.
✓Google Maps route link with stops in capture order.
✓Numbered point list for quick item lookup without confusion.
✓Large per-point links that are easy to tap on mobile.
