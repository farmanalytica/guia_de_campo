Made for field work teams

Mark points in QGIS, open routes, and generate a field-ready PDF

Field Guide helps you register important locations during activities, open routes with Google Maps stops, and generate a mobile-ready PDF with route and point links. You can now also export and import CSV coordinates to reuse points across teams and operations.

Less rework, more clarity, and more confidence for your team.

Why use it daily

Designed for activities like area inspections with issue indicators, sampling guidance, and field-work organization.

Area inspection

Mark locations with possible issues and keep a clear record for evaluation and follow-up.

Sampling guidance

Use marked points to guide collection and make sampling more organized and objective.

Field operations

Share the PDF with your team and access locations on mobile during travel and technical visits.

Recent features

A faster flow to review points and navigate locations in order.

✓Remove last mark without deleting all work.
✓Export points to CSV with capture order and WGS84 coordinates.
✓Import CSV to rebuild map marks in seconds.
✓Open Google Maps route with all points as stops.
✓Automatic route chunking for long routes.
✓PDF save flow starts in Downloads by default.

How it works

A direct flow, without complex screens or a long learning curve.

Mark or import points

During field work, click on map or import a CSV with pre-planned points.

Adjust and sync

Quickly fix marks and export CSV to share the same route with your team.

Navigate and share

Open route in Google Maps and generate an organized PDF for mobile consultation.

CSV to standardize field work

Use CSV files to move points between planning, execution, and review without repeating manual entry.

✓Export with columns order, longitude, and latitude.
✓Import with WGS84 limit validation (latitude -90..90, longitude -180..180).
✓Decimal parsing with dot or comma for mixed data sources.
✓Post-import summary showing loaded points and ignored invalid rows.

PDF designed for real field usage

The final document is designed for quick reading with clean visuals and route/point links for on-the-move usage.

✓Area map for immediate visual context.
✓Google Maps route link with stops in capture order.
✓Numbered point list for quick item lookup without confusion.
✓Large per-point links that are easy to tap on mobile.
